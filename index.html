An experienced teacher and academic

Having just completed six years as a professional secondary school science teacher, I am continuing the educational research I developed in the classroom through a PhD at the University of Cambridge.

After completing my undergraduate degree in Biomedical Sciences at UCL, I trained to become a chemistry teacher at the University of Cambridge. There I began my research into supporting students with learning needs and language disadvantage in the science classroom. As a classroom teacher I achieved substantial value-added working with students at all levels of attainment and continued to develop my research. The pilot study I developed during my MEd at the University of Cambridge was so successful that I decided to return to university as a full-time student to continue this vital work.

As a tutor, I provide a combination of exceptional academic knowledge with a deep understanding of the psychology of learning. I have a proven track record in inspiring students to exceed the predictions placed on their performance and, importantly, their own perceptions of their abilities. Learning is sequential, and so I ensure that my students have a secure understanding at every stage, enabling them to make progress with confidence.

I am passionate about enabling children to achieve their potential and become the very best they can be. This is often seen as getting the best grades they can. Whilst I help them to achieve this, I also develop every student to be a confident, independent learner, best equipped to succeed in whatever lies ahead.

I am a fourth-generation teacher, and a third-generation secondary school science teacher. I benefit from the combined wisdom of inspirational, innovative educators in tailoring my tutoring, to meet diverse individual needs. I teach all sciences and maths from primary school to GCSE, and I teach chemistry and biology to A-level and beyond.

Whilst I successfully support students in achieving the highest grades and gaining places at the top universities, I have particular expertise in supporting the development of students:

  • ☑ with Speech, Language, and Communications Needs (which includes ASD);
  • ☑ who struggle with literacy;

Tutoring Approach

My tutoring is tailored for your child. I will equip them with the tools and skills they need to become more effective, happier learners, confident when communicating orally, or in written exams.

The support I provide includes general literacy, numeracy and oracy training. I will help your child to become as independent as possible in their learning (that is, knowing how to work best by themself, with others, and knowing when and where to seek support).

In technical terms, I use scaffolded, dialogic teaching approaches and reinforcement activities designed to embed, develop, secure, and build on new concepts. The overall experience is multi-modal, through the use of phonology, semantics and orthography, supplemented with dual-coding techniques, as appropriate. Whilst I have been at the academic cutting edge of developing excellence in teaching strategies for my students, all this simply translates into them having a productive, positive learning experience, making the best use of time.

My sessions are delivered via Skype. It is important that parents/carers are present (in the same room) during the sessions, and I encourage parents/carers to get involved in the sessions to observe how I support their child, so that they may use my approaches/reiterate my advice later.

As a separate service, I can, on request, deliver coaching sessions exclusively to parents/carers who want to develop their understanding of learning so they can best support their child with confidence and engage, more knowledgably, with their school.
